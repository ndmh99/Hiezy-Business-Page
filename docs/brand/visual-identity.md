# HIEZY Visual Identity

## Visual Direction

The HIEZY visual system should feel modern, calm, premium, and commercially effective. It should combine the confidence of a digital service brand with the warmth of a business partner who explains things clearly.

The current site already points in the right direction:

- bold sans-serif display typography
- editorial serif contrast for warmth and sophistication
- a signature sky-blue brand accent with darker blue used as support
- soft white surfaces with subtle blur and glow
- high contrast between dark ink and light paper backgrounds

## Logo System

### Primary Mark

The primary logo is the HIEZY wordmark at [assets/images/brand/hiezy-logo.png](assets/images/brand/hiezy-logo.png).

### Logo Characteristics

- lowercase wordmark creates approachability
- bold, wide letterforms create confidence
- logo blue `#0EA5E9` establishes a digital-forward brand signal

### Usage Rules

- Use the full wordmark as the default choice.
- Preserve original proportions at all times.
- Keep generous clear space around the logo.
- Prefer solid light or solid dark backgrounds with strong contrast.

### Clear Space

Minimum clear space should equal the height of the logo dot above the "i" on all sides.

### Minimum Size

- Digital full logo: 120px wide minimum
- Favicon/app icon: use the dedicated favicon asset instead of shrinking the full wordmark

### Do Not

- stretch or compress the mark
- recolor the mark inconsistently across channels
- place the mark on busy photography without a solid holding shape
- add shadows, outlines, bevels, or decorative effects
- recreate the logo manually with live text

## Color System

### Core Palette

The implementation-facing palette already in use in [css/style.css](/Users/hieunguyen/Downloads/HIEZY_Web_Solutions_—_Affordable_Websites_and_Softwares_for_Small_to_Medium_Businesses/css/style.css) should remain the base system:

- Ink: `#0B1220`
- Paper: `#FBFBFC`
- Slate: `#64748B`
- Slate 2: `#475569`
- Border: `rgba(15, 23, 42, 0.10)`
- Brand / Logo Blue: `#0EA5E9`
- Supporting Action Blue: `#1D4ED8`

### Color Roles

- `Ink` is for headlines, key body copy, and high-trust UI elements.
- `Paper` is the base canvas color.
- `Slate` and `Slate 2` are for secondary information and supportive UI copy.
- `Brand / Logo Blue` is the signature brand color and should be the reference blue in brand-facing materials.
- `Supporting Action Blue` is used when a darker, more forceful UI action color is needed.

### Recommended Ratios

- 70% light neutrals
- 20% dark ink and slate structure
- 10% blue accents

### Color Behavior

- Use blue to direct attention, not to decorate everything.
- Reserve saturated blue fills for CTAs, key highlights, and trust moments.
- Keep background fields soft and breathable.
- Use gradients subtly, usually as glow, wash, or section atmosphere.

## Typography

### Primary Pairing

- Display/UI font: `Plus Jakarta Sans`
- Editorial/body accent font: `Source Serif 4`

### Type Roles

- `Plus Jakarta Sans` handles navigation, buttons, headings, labels, cards, and interface content.
- `Source Serif 4` should be used selectively to introduce warmth, sophistication, and contrast in hero copy, quotes, or highlighted value statements.

### Typography Principles

- Headlines should be concise, bold, and easy to scan.
- Serif should be used as accent, not as the dominant interface font.
- Body copy should stay highly legible with moderate line length.
- Use weight and spacing to create hierarchy before reaching for more color.

### Suggested Scale

- H1: 48-64px desktop, 36-44px mobile
- H2: 36-48px desktop, 28-36px mobile
- H3: 24-32px
- Body large: 18-20px
- Body default: 16px
- Small/supporting text: 12-14px

## Layout Language

### Core Layout Traits

- strong headline blocks
- clear section rhythms
- generous whitespace
- rounded cards and containers
- layered but not crowded compositions

### Grid Guidance

- Use a structured grid with clear alignment.
- Pair one strong conversion column with one visual support column in hero sections.
- Keep content width controlled for readability.
- Avoid dense multi-column layouts on mobile.

### Corners And Surfaces

- Primary radius: 16px to 24px
- Secondary radius: 10px to 14px
- Use soft glass or lightly tinted white surfaces for depth
- Avoid harsh shadows or sharp enterprise-style panels

## Imagery

### Image Style

Imagery should feel polished, bright, and aspirational, with enough realism to support trust.

### Preferred Image Characteristics

- clean environments
- real people or believable product views
- soft light and open compositions
- visible business outcomes, not abstract stock cliches

### Avoid

- generic office stock photography
- overly saturated startup illustrations
- dark, moody, tech-noir scenes
- cluttered photography with unclear focal points

## Iconography

- Use clean, simple line or light solid icons.
- Icons should support comprehension, not become the visual centerpiece.
- Keep icon styling consistent within one interface.

## Motion

- Motion should feel smooth and reassuring.
- Prefer subtle float, reveal, and hover states over loud animation.
- Motion should reinforce hierarchy, not distract from content.

## Accessibility Standards

- Maintain WCAG-conscious contrast for all key text and CTAs.
- Never rely on color alone to communicate meaning.
- Ensure focus states are obvious and visible.
- Keep interactive targets comfortably tappable on mobile.
- Avoid overly light gray text for important information.
