# HIEZY Voice And Messaging

## Voice Summary

HIEZY should sound clear, capable, warm, and commercially grounded. The voice should feel like an experienced digital partner who understands both aesthetics and business outcomes.

## Tone Attributes

- clear, not clever-first
- confident, not pushy
- warm, not casual
- professional, not corporate
- simple, not simplistic

## Writing Principles

- Lead with practical value.
- Use plain language over technical jargon.
- Make promises specific and believable.
- Reduce anxiety with clarity and structure.
- Keep copy action-oriented.

## Messaging Pillars

### Affordable Professionalism

HIEZY delivers polished digital work without the traditional agency overhead.

### Straightforward Process

Clients should always know what happens next, what is included, and what they own.

### Ownership And Independence

The brand should repeatedly reinforce that clients keep control of their website, files, and platform decisions.

### Ongoing Support

Support is a differentiator. It should be framed as dependable and practical, not as a vague promise.

## Headline Style

Headlines should be:

- short
- benefit-led
- easy to scan in under 3 seconds
- written for decision-making, not awards juries

### Good Headline Patterns

- Professional websites that are easy to launch and manage
- Clear pricing for businesses that need to move fast
- Design that helps your business look credible from day one
- Simple digital systems that support real growth

### Patterns To Avoid

- empty superlatives
- startup buzzwords
- vague transformation language
- overly poetic headlines that hide the offer

## CTA Language

CTA copy should feel active, helpful, and low-friction.

### Preferred CTA Patterns

- See our packages
- Book a talk
- Request a quote
- Start your website
- View portfolio

### Avoid

- Submit
- Learn more as the only CTA everywhere
- aggressive urgency language
- hype-based CTA copy

## Copy Structure

### Best Practice Formula

1. State the value.
2. Clarify who it is for.
3. Explain what is included.
4. Remove a common fear.
5. Offer a next step.

### Example

Build a professional website for your business with clear pricing, full ownership, and launch support. Ideal for teams that need to look credible quickly without getting locked into an agency relationship.

## Approved Brand Vocabulary

- professional
- clear
- honest
- launch
- support
- ownership
- custom
- polished
- easy to manage
- built to perform

## Vocabulary To Limit

- disrupt
- revolutionary
- bleeding-edge
- world-class
- premium solution
- synergize
- leverage as filler

## Service Descriptions

Service copy should answer:

- What is this?
- Who is it for?
- What business problem does it solve?
- What do I get?
- What happens after launch?

## Testimonials And Social Proof

When writing or editing testimonials, prioritize:

- trust
- clarity
- reliability
- speed
- professionalism

## Microcopy Standards

- Form labels should be clear and direct.
- Helper text should remove uncertainty.
- Empty states should be useful, not cute.
- Error messages should explain what to fix in plain language.
- Success messages should confirm what happens next.

## Tone By Context

### Homepage

Confident, concise, conversion-focused.

### Pricing

Explicit, calm, reassuring.

### Contact Forms

Friendly, low-pressure, helpful.

### Support Or Post-Launch Content

Steady, practical, solutions-oriented.
